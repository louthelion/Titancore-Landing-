Business System Intelligence explains how the company’s internal control system connects requests, routing, departments, approvals, workflow, dashboards, documents, contracts, calendars, follow-up, permissions, compliance, and leadership visibility.

This page is different from Data Intelligence. Data Intelligence studies numbers and signals. Business System Intelligence studies the structure and control system that moves work through the company.

Main Definition

Business System Intelligence is the internal control map for how business work moves.

What this page is for

This page is the control hub for business systems. It explains how work enters the company, how it is routed, who reviews it, where it is stored, how approvals happen, how departments stay connected, and how leadership sees what needs action.

The corrected system title is System Reports & Controls. The Data Intelligence page keeps Reports & Dashboards. This prevents the two sections from having the same card title.

Business System Intelligence Focuses on internal systems, ownership, access, routing, approvals, controls, dashboard connections, and workflow movement.
Data Intelligence Focuses on numbers, reports, dashboards, warning signs, market signals, customer signals, business numbers, and decision review.

System Control Map

A complete system should show entry, routing, review, approval, storage, and reporting.

Entry points Forms, calls, emails, messages, applications, service requests, contracts, reports, and leadership tasks.
Routing points Company routing, department routing, email routing, call routing, document routing, and approval routing.
Control points Dashboard permissions, department ownership, approval monitoring, quality checks, compliance review, and audit logs.
Visibility points System reports, control panels, leadership dashboards, meeting calendars, activity logs, and follow-up tracking.
