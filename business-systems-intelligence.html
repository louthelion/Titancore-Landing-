Business Systems Overview

A system is the structure behind the work.

A business system is not only software. It is the full path the work follows. A good system tells people what to collect, where to send it, who reviews it, how to follow up, where to save it, and what system report should show the result.

Forms & Intake

Forms and intake collect the information a business needs.

Intake is the first part of the system. If the business does not collect the right information at the beginning, the rest of the process becomes slower, weaker, and more confusing.

Name and contact information.
Business or organization name if needed.
Type of request or topic.
Urgency, timeline, or location.
Documents, notes, or supporting information.
Preferred response method and next step.
Routing & Follow-Up

Routing makes sure the request goes to the right place.

Routing prevents messages from sitting in the wrong place. A request should not be lost because it went to the wrong inbox, wrong department, wrong person, or wrong page.

Which request goes to public contact?
Which request goes to a company page?
Which request needs a form?
Which request needs document review?
Who is responsible for responding?
What happens if the request is incomplete?

Reviews & Approvals

Important work should be reviewed before it is final.

Reviews and approvals protect the company from mistakes. They confirm that information is complete, the right steps were followed, and the final work is ready before it is sent, filed, published, or completed.

Is the information complete?
Is the request assigned correctly?
Was the correct process followed?
Is anything missing or unclear?
Does a reviewer need to approve it?
Was the final result documented?
Records & Archives

Records keep the company from losing important information.

A business needs organized records so people can understand what happened, when it happened, who handled it, what decision was made, and what needs to happen next.

Submitted forms and inquiries.
Emails and important messages.
Documents and attachments.
Approval notes and review comments.
Reports and summaries.
Final decisions or completion notes.
System Reports & Controls

System reports and controls help leadership see how the internal system is moving.

System Reports & Controls should show what is coming in, what is being routed, what is being worked on, what is delayed, what needs approval, what is complete, and what needs leadership attention inside the business system.

New activity and new system requests.
Pending work and assigned system work.
Delayed work and follow-up needed.
Approvals, corrections, and review items.
Completed work and saved system records.
Leadership control and attention items.
Automation Readiness

A business should organize the system before adding automation.

Automation works better when the process is already clear. Before a company automates, it should know what information is needed, where it goes, who reviews it, what conditions matter, and what result should happen.

Is the intake form clear?
Are categories defined?
Is routing clear?
Are review steps clear?
Are approval rules clear?
Are records and system reports needed?
