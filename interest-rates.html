Interest Rate Definition

Interest rates show the cost of borrowing money.

Interest rates affect how much people and businesses pay to borrow. They influence mortgages, credit cards, car loans, business loans, real estate decisions, bank lending, investor behavior, and customer confidence.

Mortgage RatesRates affect monthly payments, buyer qualification, refinancing, housing demand, and real estate confidence.
Credit CardsHigher rates can make balances more expensive and increase pressure on households.
Business LoansRates affect funding, expansion, inventory, equipment purchases, and working capital.
Auto LoansRates affect vehicle payments, buyer approvals, dealership demand, and household budgets.
Investor BehaviorRates can shift money between stocks, bonds, real estate, cash, and safer investments.
Bank LendingRates influence loan standards, credit availability, deposits, and financial confidence.

Interest Rate Warning Signs

Rate pressure can slow decisions before people realize why.

Interest rates affect affordability. When borrowing becomes expensive, consumers may delay purchases, businesses may delay expansion, investors may become cautious, and banks may tighten approvals.

Payments riseLoans, mortgages, cards, and financing become more expensive each month.
Approvals become harderHigher payments can make borrowers qualify for less money.
Housing demand slowsBuyers may delay purchases when rates increase monthly costs.
Business expansion pausesCompanies may avoid new loans, equipment, inventory, or locations.
Credit card stress increasesHigher rates can make balances harder to pay down.
Investors become cautiousMarkets may move carefully when rate direction is uncertain.
Banks tighten lendingLenders may require stronger income, credit, collateral, or documents.
Customers delay decisionsPeople may wait before buying homes, cars, services, or major items.
