Market Research Overview

What should be checked before entering a market?

A business should not enter a market only because the idea sounds good. Market research checks whether the opportunity is real, whether people need the service, whether they will pay, and whether the business can compete.

Core market questions

  • How many people need this service?
  • Who is already providing it?
  • What do customers complain about?
  • Is the market growing, stable, or getting weaker?
  • What makes this business different?
  • Can the business make money after expenses?
Market Research Areas

The main areas every business should review.

Demand

Demand means people actually need the product or service. A business should check how many customers need it, how often they need it, and whether the need is strong enough to support steady sales.

Pricing

Pricing shows whether customers are willing to pay and whether the business can make profit. The company should compare competitor prices, customer expectations, service quality, expenses, and profit margin.

Location

Location shows where demand exists. Some businesses depend on local traffic, nearby customers, population, business activity, or service coverage. Online businesses still need to know what region or audience they are targeting.

Competition

Competition shows who is already serving the market. A business should review competitor services, pricing, reviews, complaints, strengths, weaknesses, and what customers wish was better.

Customer Problems

Customer problems show why people might buy. The stronger the problem, the stronger the opportunity. Research should identify what customers are struggling with and what solution they are looking for.

Market Strength

Market strength shows whether the market is growing, stable, crowded, risky, or weakening. Strong markets have demand, customer spending, repeat need, and room for better service.

Timing

Timing matters because a good idea can fail if the market is not ready. Research should check economic conditions, seasonal demand, interest rates, customer spending, technology changes, and legal changes.

Risk

Risk shows what can go wrong. A business should check startup cost, monthly expenses, legal requirements, customer trust, competition, market changes, and whether the business can survive slow months.

Opportunity

Opportunity exists where demand is strong but service is weak, expensive, slow, confusing, or missing. A better system, better customer experience, or better trust can create a business opening.

Question Guide

Market research questions visitors can use.

Before spending money, ask:

  • Who exactly is the customer?
  • What problem does the customer need solved?
  • How many people in the area need this?
  • How often will customers buy or use this service?
  • What are customers already paying?
  • Who are the top competitors?
  • What do customers like about those competitors?
  • What do customers complain about?
  • Can a new company do something better?
  • Is the market large enough for another business?
  • What licenses, permits, or rules may apply?
  • What startup costs and monthly costs should be expected?
  • What could make the business fail?
  • What could make the business grow?
