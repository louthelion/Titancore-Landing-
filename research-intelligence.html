Market Research

Market research studies the environment around a business idea.

Before a person opens a business or expands a service, they should review market size, customer demand, pricing, location, industry movement, and whether the market is growing, stable, or under pressure.

Market research questions

What should be checked?

How many people need this service? Who is already providing it? What do customers complain about? Is the market getting stronger or weaker? What makes this business different?

Customer Research

Customer research explains who the business is trying to serve.

A business becomes stronger when it understands its customer. Customer research includes needs, pain points, buying habits, income level, urgency, trust, service expectations, and how the customer makes decisions.

Customer research questions

What should be understood?

Who is the customer? What problem do they need solved? How often do they need the service? What makes them trust a company? What makes them walk away?

Competitor Research

Competitor research shows what already exists in the market.

Competitor research helps business owners understand pricing, service quality, reputation, weakness, strengths, marketing, customer complaints, and open space in the market.

Competitor research questions

What should be compared?

Who are the main competitors? What do they offer? What do they charge? What are customers saying about them? What can a new company do better?

Opportunity Search

Opportunity search looks for gaps and future growth.

Opportunity search is about finding where demand exists but service is weak, expensive, slow, confusing, or missing. It also looks at new trends, local changes, new customer needs, and under-served markets.
