System Reports & Controls

System Reports & Controls explain how internal systems help leadership track new work, pending work, delays, approvals, completed work, records, and attention items.

A strong system report does more than show numbers. It shows what is happening inside the business, what needs action, what is waiting, what is late, what was approved, what was completed, and what should be reviewed before the company loses control of the workflow.

Main Definition

Reports show what is happening. Controls show what needs action.

A system report is a clear view of activity inside the business. It can show new requests, open assignments, approval needs, follow-up items, delayed work, completed records, and final outcomes.

A system control is a rule, checkpoint, warning, or review point that helps the business respond before work becomes lost, ignored, duplicated, delayed, incomplete, or completed incorrectly.

Reports create visibility They help leadership see activity without searching through emails, forms, calls, notes, and scattered files.
Controls create discipline They tell the business when to review, correct, approve, escalate, close, or reopen work.
Reports track movement They show what entered the system, where it went, who owns it, and what is waiting.
Controls protect the process They reduce missed follow-up, wrong routing, weak approvals, incomplete records, and delayed action.

Report Areas

A useful report should make the business easier to understand.

Report 01

New Work

Shows new forms, calls, emails, messages, applications, documents, or tasks that entered the system.

Report 02

Pending Work

Shows work that has started but is not finished, including assignments waiting on review or response.

Report 03

Delayed Work

Shows requests or tasks that are taking too long, missing information, or waiting on action.

Report 04

Approval Items

Shows work waiting for review, correction, approval, leadership decision, or final confirmation.

Report 05

Completed Work

Shows what was finished, when it was completed, who handled it, and where the final record is saved.

Report 06

Attention Items

Shows what needs leadership attention, correction, follow-up, escalation, or a decision before moving forward.

Control Areas

Controls help the business know when something needs action.

Routing control Confirms that each request goes to the correct company, department, page, inbox, or review path.
Follow-up control Confirms that requests are not left unanswered, ignored, forgotten, or waiting too long.
Approval control Confirms that important work is reviewed and approved before it is considered final.
Record control Confirms that final documents, messages, notes, decisions, and completion proof are saved.
Delay control Identifies tasks that are taking too long, waiting on someone, or missing required information.
Correction control Identifies work that must be fixed before it can be approved, filed, delivered, or closed.
Leadership control Shows what needs higher-level attention, decision-making, approval, or review.
Completion control Confirms that work is actually finished and not only marked complete too early.
Warning Signs

System reports should make weak points visible early.

A report becomes powerful when it shows problems while they are still small. Warning signs help leadership notice delay, confusion, repeated mistakes, missing records, weak approvals, and unanswered requests before they damage the business.

Too many pending items Work is entering the system faster than it is being reviewed or completed.
Too many delays Requests are getting stuck because ownership, routing, review, or approval is unclear.
Missing records Work is being completed without proof, notes, final documents, or saved decisions.
Repeated corrections The same mistake is happening again and again because the process has not been fixed.
Unanswered follow-up Customers, partners, or internal teams are waiting too long for response.
Approval bottlenecks Work is waiting too long because review or signature rules are not moving clearly.
Leadership Review

Reports should help leadership make better decisions.

Leadership should not only see finished work. Leadership should also see the movement of the system: what came in, what is open, what is late, what needs correction, what needs approval, what was completed, and what should be watched closely.

Daily review Look at new requests, urgent items, delayed items, and unanswered follow-up.
Weekly review Look at volume, completion, repeated problems, pending approvals, and department movement.
Monthly review Look at patterns, system weaknesses, customer signals, performance, risk, and improvement areas.
Exception review Look at anything outside the normal process: high risk, missed deadlines, missing records, or unresolved issues.
Questions To Ask

Use these questions when reviewing system reports and controls.

What came into the system?

Review new forms, calls, emails, documents, applications, messages, and requests.

Where did it go?

Confirm the request was routed to the correct place and not left in the wrong inbox or page.

Who owns it?

Every item should have a person, department, company, or process path responsible for the next step.

What is waiting?

Separate work waiting on documents, customer response, staff review, leadership approval, or correction.

What is late?

Find anything that has passed the expected response time, review time, approval time, or completion time.

What was approved?

Check what was reviewed, who approved it, when it was approved, and what record was saved.

What was completed?

Confirm final completion, saved records, completion notes, and next-step closure.

What needs leadership attention?

Identify risk, delay, repeated problems, missing information, approval issues, and unresolved items.
